About Me

I'm a third-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts-Amherst. My research interests lie in the intersection of causal inference and simulation. I'm fortunate to be advised by David Jensen and Peter Haas. I have also worked on research involving disease modeling and risk assessment for communities in Massachusetts.

Prior to this, I worked at Cisco Systems (India) as a systems engineer. I was responsible for solution design and testing for routing, switching, wireless, and data center architectures. I also contributed to developing network programmability solutions for various technology. I have an undergraduate degree in electronics and telecommunication engineering.

Research

My research lies in the intersection of causal inference and simulation. My goal is to build explainable simulation metamodels using causal inference. I also work on improving evaluation of causal algorithms by developing new types of data that result in a more comprehensive and fair evaluation of estimators.
